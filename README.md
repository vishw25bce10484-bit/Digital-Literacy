Digital Literacy & Professional Development Toolkit

📌 Overview

This repository contains a collection of five distinct projects focused on establishing a strong, secure, and professional digital presence. From designing visual awareness campaigns to coding a personal web portfolio and analyzing cybersecurity threats, this compilation serves as a comprehensive guide to navigating the digital landscape as a computer science student and future software engineer.


🗂️ Project Modules

1. Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
A visual guide designed to educate peers on the fundamentals of digital literacy. It breaks down complex concepts like data privacy, online footprint management, and information verification into an easily digestible and engaging format.


2. Student Digital Portfolio
A fully customized, graphical motioned personal website built to showcase academic achievements, technical skills, and software projects.
Focus Areas: Web Development, UI/UX, and highlighting core competencies in Python, C++, and AI/Machine Learning.

3. Explore Coding & Collaboration Platforms
An exploration of industry-standard version control and collaborative coding environments. This section covers best practices for using platforms like GitHub, managing repositories, writing effective commit messages, and handling pull requests to ensure seamless teamwork on development projects.



4. Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
A structured handbook for digital professionalism.



Templates: Includes ready-to-use drafts for academic extension requests and corporate internship inquiries.
Social Media Checklist: Outlines the critical Do's and Don'ts of maintaining a professional online presence to protect future career opportunities.
5. Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide
An in-depth analysis of modern social engineering threats, focusing specifically on UPI Payment Fraud via screen-mirroring applications.



Case Study: A step-by-step breakdown of how the crime is executed and its consequences.
Prevention Checklist: Actionable security protocols, including 2FA implementation, principle of least privilege, and official reporting channels (Helpline 1930 / cybercrime.gov.in).
